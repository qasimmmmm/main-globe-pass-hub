What is ESTA?

The Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA) is an essential requirement for citizens of countries participating in the Visa Waiver Program (VWP). Implemented in 2009 by the United States Government, ESTA allows travelers to visit the U.S. for tourism, business, medical purposes, or transit to another destination without needing a conventional visa. This permit is valid for two years and allows multiple entries, with stays of up to 90 days per visit.

How to apply for ESTA?

Initial Requirements: Ensure your passport is an electronic passport or “e-Passport”, as this type of passport contains a chip required for the VWP.

Complete the Form: Fill out the online application form accurately. Errors in the information may cause delays or denial of your authorization.

Fee Payment: The application process includes a total fee of $21, consisting of a $4 processing charge and a $17 authorization fee if your application is approved.

Receive the Authorization: Once submitted, you will receive a response within 24 to 48 hours. Be sure to apply at least 72 hours before your trip.

Important Considerations

Validity and Renewal: ESTA is valid for two years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. To travel again after expiration, you must apply for a new authorization.

Purpose of Travel: ESTA can only be used for tourism, business, or transit. It is not valid for other purposes such as studying or working.

Data Protection: All information provided is securely stored and will not be shared with third parties without your consent.
