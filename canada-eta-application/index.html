WHAT IS THE eTA CANADA?

The Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) is a mandatory entry requirement for citizens of visa-exempt countries traveling to Canada by air. Introduced by Canadian government in 2016, it allows travelers to visit Canada for tourism, business, or transit without a traditional visa. The eTA is electronically linked to your passport and is valid for up to 5 years or until your passport expires.

Important Considerations

Validity and Renewal: eTA is valid for two years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. To travel again after expiration, you must apply for a new authorization.

Purpose of Travel: eTA can only be used for tourism, business, or transit. It is not valid for other purposes such as studying or working.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the eTA?
The eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) is a mandatory travel document for visa-exempt foreign nationals flying to Canada. It allows short stays for tourism, business, or transit without the need for a traditional visa.
Who can apply for an eTA?
Citizens of visa-exempt countries who plan to travel to Canada by air. It is also required for passengers transiting through a Canadian airport. U.S. citizens do not need an eTA, but U.S. permanent residents do.
How long is the eTA valid?
The eTA is valid for up to 5 years, or until your passport expires—whichever comes first. You can use it for multiple trips, with stays of up to six months per visit.
How long does an eTA take to process?
Most applications are approved within minutes. However, some cases may require additional processing, which can take up to 72 hours.
Is an eTA required for children?
Yes. Every traveler, including infants and minors, must have their own approved eTA when flying to Canada.
Does an eTA guarantee entry to Canada?
No. An approved eTA allows you to board a flight to Canada, but final entry is determined by a border services officer upon arrival.
