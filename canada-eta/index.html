What is the Canada eTA?

An electronic authorization linked to your passport that allows you to fly to Canada or connect at a Canadian airport without a physical visa.

5-Year Validity

With a single eTA you can enter and exit Canada as many times as you want for 5 years, or until your passport expires.

Approval in Minutes

Most applications receive automatic approval almost immediately. You will receive your eTA directly by email.

Linked to Your Passport

No physical stamp or sticker. The eTA is electronically linked to your biometric passport automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs an eTA to travel to Canada?

Citizens of visa-exempt countries who travel to Canada by air: EU citizens (including those from the UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain), Australians, Japanese, South Koreans, and many more. US citizens with an American passport do NOT need an eTA.

How long is the Canada eTA valid?

The eTA is valid for 5 years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. It allows multiple trips to Canada, with stays of up to 6 months per visit as determined by the border officer.

Do I need an eTA if I am only connecting through Canada?

Yes. An eTA is mandatory even if you are only transiting through a Canadian airport and do not plan to leave the transit area. You must obtain it before boarding your departing flight.

When should I apply for an eTA?

You can apply at any time before your flight. We recommend applying at least 72 hours in advance. With our urgent service, we can process it in 1–2 hours if necessary.

What happens if my eTA is rejected?

If your application is rejected, we will fully refund our processing fee and advise you on the next steps, which typically involve applying for a visitor visa at the Canadian embassy.
